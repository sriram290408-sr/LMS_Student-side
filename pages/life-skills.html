What are 10 basic life skills?

The 10 core life skills, as proposed by the World Health Organization (WHO), focus on personal and social well-being, including Self-awareness, Empathy, Critical Thinking, Creative Thinking, Problem Solving, Decision Making, Coping with Stress, Coping with Emotions, Effective Communication, and Healthy Interpersonal Relationships. These skills help individuals navigate life's challenges, build strong connections, and make sound choices.

What are life skills?

Life skills are essential abilities for managing daily life effectively, helping individuals handle challenges, make good decisions, and interact positively with others to achieve well-being and success in school, work, and society, encompassing cognitive (critical thinking, problem-solving), personal/emotional (self-awareness, stress management), and interpersonal (communication, empathy) competencies that are learned and developed

What are the 7 soft skills?

While there's no single definitive list, the 7 most commonly cited essential soft skills for career success are Communication, Teamwork, Problem-Solving, Adaptability, Emotional Intelligence, Time Management, and Leadership, focusing on how you work, collaborate, and handle challenges to become a standout professional

What are 10 key skills?

The "10 key skills" usually refer to essential soft and hard skills for career success, often including Communication, Problem-Solving, Teamwork, Time Management, Adaptability, Critical Thinking, Leadership, Initiative, Digital Literacy, and Creativity, though specific lists vary by source and industry, focusing on traits that help you navigate challenges and add value in the workplace.

What are 5 personal skills examples?

Five essential personal skills examples are Communication, Problem-Solving, Time Management, Adaptability, and Teamwork, which highlight your ability to work effectively with others, manage tasks, think critically, and adjust to new situations, making you a valuable asset in any role.
